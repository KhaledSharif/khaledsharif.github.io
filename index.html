Khaled Sharif

Robotics engineer at NASA Ames. Born in London. Interested in machine learning and computer simulations. Occasional swimmer, biker, and baker. Check out my research, projects, and publications, or scroll down.

About Myself

I graduated from the University of Jordan as a computer engineer; my Bachleor's thesis investigated the use of ensemble based machine learning algorithms on stock market prediction - the code and paper are both publicly available.

After graduation, I worked as a data scientist at ArabiaWeather, the Arab world's largest private weather forecasting company; I designed and built automatic weather prediction machine learning models, ran model verification tests, and enhanced existing simulation models to better suit the company's needs. I also worked on Jordan's first satellite: I was the lead engineer for the satellite's on board computer.

Now I'm a robotics engineer at NASA's Ames Research Center in California. As part of the Intelligent Robotics Group, we conduct applied research in computer vision, geospatial data systems, human-robot interaction, planetary mapping, and robot software.
