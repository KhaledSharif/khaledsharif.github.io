Research and Experience

In this section I'll list my current research and work experience. I chose to group these two categories as one as most of my work has involved research. This section contains my work building Jordan's first satellite, improving weather forecasting for the Arab world, and forecasting earthquakes at NASA.

Reaching Low Earth Orbit

I am proud to be part of the team working on "JY1-SAT" — Jordan’s first satellite — named in memory of His Majesty the late King Hussein (who held the radio call sign of JY1). I am the lead engineer for the satellite's on-board computer: the main control unit of the satellite. The satellite will be launched into space during the first quarter of 2018, and will remain in low earth orbit for at least two years.

The satellite, built in collaboration with the Radio Amateur Satellite Corporation, will be a Ham radio transponder and broadcaster, occasionally broadcasting a message recorded by the Crown Prince of Jordan. More importantly, however, the satellite is Jordan's foray into space and satellite technologies. The lessons learned from building JY1-SAT will be used to found a Jordanian space initiative for building satellites and exploring space.

Forecasting Earthquakes

During my research internship at the NASA Ames Research Center , I studied the field of geology and the science behind earthquakes; this knowledge was used to better understand how earthquake precursor signals can be used to forecast earthquakes automatically. I was Professor Friedemann Freund's research assistant and applied his theory of earthquake precursor signals to find information buried deep in the low frequencies of electromagnetic sensor data captured by a NASA team in Alaska.

Additionally, in concert with a team of NASA interns and Patrick Hogan, we developed NASA World Weather : the web's largest three-dimensional geospatial interactive platform for weather and satellite data. NASA World Weather is hosted publicly on NASA's website and is regularly maintained. It was also the recipient of the 1st place award at NASA's Europa Challenge in 2017 in Helsinki.

Improving Prediction Accuracy

At ArabiaWeather, I worked as a research scientist. My tasks included designing and building numerical weather prediction models, most of which used machine learning at their core, to help maintain ArabiaWeather's status as the most accurate weather forecasting company for the region.

Through the company, we have released many contributions to the field of meteorological science. Bader is a leading weather forecast visualization platform. Athena is a machine learning tool that helps researchers fit mathematical equations to their data. I also took part in the International Conference on Reanalaysis to present our work on improving the accuracy of meteorological reanalysis.
